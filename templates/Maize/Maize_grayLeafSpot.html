Gray Leaf Spot


It is a fungal disease that affects various grass species, including maize
(corn), turfgrasses, and other cereals. It is caused by the fungus
Cercospora zeae-maydis. Gray leaf spot can significantly impact crop
yields and turf quality, making it an important concern for agricultural
and turf grass industries.

More Insights


Gray leaf spot is caused by the fungal pathogen Cercospora zeae-maydis.
It thrives under warm and humid conditions, often occurring when
temperatures range from 25 to 30°C (77 to 86°F). Prolonged leaf wetness
due to rain or irrigation further promotes disease development.
Overcrowding, excessive nitrogen fertilization, and poor air circulation
contribute to severity.


Detect gray leaf spot through visual inspection of lower leaves. Look
for small, tan lesions near the leaf collar that elongate and turn
grayish-brown with a rectangular shape. Yellow halos may surround the
lesions. Regular scouting and monitoring help identify early signs of
the disease.


Prevent gray leaf spot by implementing cultural practices such as crop
rotation, using resistant maize hybrids, proper spacing, and irrigation
management. Avoid excessive leaf wetness and promote good air
circulation. Fungicides may be necessary in severe cases, following
recommended guidelines. Timely action and integrated pest management
practices are crucial for effective management.

Rapid Remedies


Cultural practices:
▲Crop rotation: Avoid planting maize in consecutive seasons to break the disease cycle and reduce pathogen buildup.
▲Proper spacing and pruning: Provide adequate spacing between plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity levels, limiting the favorable conditions for disease development.
▲Irrigation management: Avoid overhead irrigation that can promote leaf wetness. Instead, utilize drip irrigation or soaker hoses to deliver water directly to the soil.
▲Nutrient management: Maintain balanced fertilization practices to promote plant health and minimize susceptibility to diseases.
Resistant varieties:
▲Plant maize hybrids or turf grass cultivars that are resistant or tolerant to gray leaf spot. Consult with local agricultural experts or seed suppliers to identify suitable resistant varieties for your region.
Fungicide applications:
▲In severe cases or when cultural practices alone are insufficient, consider applying fungicides following local regulations and recommendations. It is important to select appropriate fungicides effective against gray leaf spot and follow the recommended application rates and timing.
Removal of infected plant material:
▲Regularly scout and remove infected plant debris or leaves to reduce the source of inoculum and limit disease spread.
Monitoring and early detection:
▲Regularly monitor the crop or turf grass for signs of gray leaf spot. Be vigilant in identifying initial symptoms and take prompt action to prevent further spread.
